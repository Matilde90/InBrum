Why Birmingham?

Chinese English French Hindi Italian
Birmingham is the largest city of the West Midlands, and the second most populous city of the United Kingdom with roughly 1,14 million inhabitants. It lies approximately 110 miles (177km) northwest of London. ‘City of a Thousand Trades,’ it was a thriving center during the Industrial revolution and still remains a manufacturing and engineering focus nowadays. People from Birmingham are called Brummies, a term derived from the city’s nickname of 'Brum,' which originates from the city’s old name, Brummagem.

J. R. R. Tolkien
From the age of four, J. R. R. Tolkien grew up by the fields and mill at Sarehole, a village now absorbed into Birmingham’s suburbs, from which he took inspiration for ‘The Shire’ (The Hobbit). Perrott’s Folly and the Waterworks Tower in Edgbaston, as well as Old Joe, the University of Birmingham’s clock tower, were his sources of inspiration for the tower of Orthanc, the black tower of Isengard (The Lord of the Rings).

Green Oasis
With 571 parks, Birmingham is one of the UK’s greenest cities. Sutton Park, 6 miles north of the city centre, is one of the largest urban parks in Europe and is designated as a National Nature Reserve and a Site of Special Scientific Interest.

The Lunar Society
In the 18th century, Birmingham paid homage to the richest man in the world back then: Matthew Boulton. Boulton was the manufacturer and engineer who financed and introduced James Watt’s steam engine. He built the Soho manufactory near Birmingham and lived in the elegant Soho House where he hosted meetings of the Lunar Society.

Venice of the North
With nearly 35 miles of canals, Birmingham has more canals than Venice!

Heavy Metal
Birmingham is the birthplace of heavy metal bands Back Sabbath and Judas Priest. Black Sabbath was formed in 1968 by Tony Iommi, Geezer Butler, Bill Ward and Ozzy Osbourne –their played their last concert in the city that saw them rise in February 2017. Judas Priest was formed in 1969 in industrial West Bromwich, 10km west of Birmingham. After many band member replacements, they are still rocking!

Cinema
In the Southside district of Birmingham one can find the UK’s oldest working cinema: the Electric. Nowadays the cinema has two digital screens and shows a mixture of the latest quality mainstream, independent, foreign and classic films.
